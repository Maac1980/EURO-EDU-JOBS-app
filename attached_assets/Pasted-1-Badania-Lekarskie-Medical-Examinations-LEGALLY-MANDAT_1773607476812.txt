1. Badania Lekarskie (Medical Examinations) — LEGALLY MANDATORY
Under Kodeks Pracy Art. 229, every worker must have a valid medical clearance — wstępne (initial), okresowe (periodic), kontrolne (follow-up after sick leave). Currently not tracked at all. A PIP inspector asking for this would immediately flag every worker as non-compliant.

2. Oświadczenie o Powierzeniu Pracy (Work Declaration)
For non-EU workers from Ukraine, Belarus, Georgia, Moldova, Armenia, Russia — employers must file an Oświadczenie with the local Powiatowy Urząd Pracy (PUP). These expire (typically 6–24 months). Not tracked. This is the #1 legal exposure for Polish welding subcontractors.

3. EN ISO 9606 Welding Certification Details
You track that a worker is a "welder" but not what they're certified to weld. In Polish industrial practice, the client always demands the specific certification: process (MIG/MAG/TIG/MMA), material group, thickness range, position, gas. Without this, you can't prove the right welder is on the right job.

4. PESEL / NIP
Essential for ZUS registration, tax filings, and PIP inspections. Every worker record should have this for legal identification.

5. ZUS Registration Status
Is the worker actually registered in ZUS (Social Insurance)? An employer with even one unregistered worker faces massive fines. There should be a ZUS status flag.

6. UDT Certificates
If workers operate lifting equipment, pressure vessels, or scaffolding — Urząd Dozoru Technicznego certification is required and has a specific expiry date.

7. Visa Type Tracking
A worker on a Type C Schengen tourist visa cannot work legally. The system should distinguish visa types (A/C/D, Karta Pobytu type: permanent/temporary/EU long-term).

8. RODO Consent
Under Polish GDPR implementation, storing biometric/ID data without explicit consent records is a legal risk. No consent tracking exists.
