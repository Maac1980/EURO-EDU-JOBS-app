Not quite — PIT-2 is a benefit purely for the employee, not the employer. Here's how it works:

PIT-2 declaration (filed by the worker):

The worker tells you (the employer) "you are my primary employer"
You then deduct 300 PLN less from their monthly tax advance
That 300 PLN stays in the worker's pocket instead of going to the tax office
Your total cost as employer stays exactly the same — you just send 300 PLN less to the Urząd Skarbowy on the worker's behalf
What changes and what doesn't:

Without PIT-2	With PIT-2
Employer's gross cost	Same	Same
Employer ZUS contributions	Same	Same
Worker's take-home	Lower	+300 PLN/month
Tax office receives	More	−300 PLN/month
Important rule in Poland: A worker can only file PIT-2 with one employer — their primary one. If a worker has two jobs (which is what the Split Employer calculator handles), they should only have PIT-2 filed with one of them, otherwise they'll owe tax at year-end reconciliation.

Where the employer does save money is through the Split Contract arrangement we have in the dashboard — when a worker's hours are split across two employers, the second employer pays no ZUS contributions at all (only PIT). That is a genuine employer-side saving. PIT-2 is separate from that.
